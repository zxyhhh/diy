# SoloDay Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026

Thank you for using SoloDay. SoloDay is an app for daily intentions, gentle planning, future notes, focus timing, weather, and reflection. We care about your privacy and aim to use only the information needed to provide the app's core features.

Before publishing, please replace "Developer name" and "Contact email" in this document with your real information.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to the SoloDay mobile app and its related features, including Daily Intentions, Do, Future, reflections, weather, moon phase, themes, iCloud sync, widgets, focus timer, system review prompts, and export or sharing features.

## 2. Information We Collect Or Process

SoloDay may store or process the following information on your device:

### 2.1 Content You Enter

This may include:

- Daily intentions
- Daily reflections
- Do items
- Future notes
- Task duration, completion status, and focus timer state
- Preferences such as theme, temperature unit, and reminder settings

This content is used to display, edit, sync, and export your records inside the app. SoloDay does not publicly display your personal records to other users.

### 2.2 Device Location

When you use weather-related features, SoloDay requests location permission to provide local weather, sunrise and sunset, UV index, precipitation, and related daily context.

Location information is used only to provide local weather and natural rhythm information. You can disable SoloDay's location permission at any time in system settings. If location permission is disabled, weather features may not work properly.

### 2.3 Weather And Moon Information

SoloDay uses your location to fetch weather data and may cache the latest weather result on your device so the app can show the most recent update when the network is unavailable. Moon phase information is usually calculated on device or displayed together with weather-related information.

### 2.4 Usage And Diagnostic Information

SoloDay uses Firebase Analytics to collect basic app usage information, such as screen views, feature usage, device and system environment, and diagnostic signals. This helps us understand whether the app is working properly and improve product quality.

We do not intentionally use analytics to collect the specific text of your daily intentions, reflections, tasks, or Future notes.

### 2.5 System Review And Sharing

SoloDay may use local app activity to decide when to request the system's native review prompt. Information used for this purpose is stored locally, such as active-day count, positive usage counters, and the last prompt time.

When you choose to export or share content, SoloDay passes the selected content to the system share sheet. The destination, channel, and further processing depend on the third-party app or system service you choose.

## 3. How We Use Information

We use the information described above to:

- Save and display your daily intentions, reflections, tasks, and Future notes
- Sync your SoloDay data across your devices
- Fetch and show location-based weather, sunrise and sunset, UV, and precipitation information
- Provide focus timer, Lock Screen, Dynamic Island, Live Activities, or widget features
- Remember preferences such as theme, temperature unit, and reminder settings
- Support exporting, sharing, and reviewing past records
- Analyze basic app usage to improve stability and experience
- Respond to feedback, support requests, or troubleshooting needs

## 4. Local Storage And iCloud Sync

SoloDay stores app data on your device so the app can work offline. SoloDay may also use Apple iCloud services to sync the following data across your devices:

- App settings
- Daily intentions
- Daily reflections
- Do items
- Future notes

iCloud sync depends on your Apple ID, iCloud settings, network status, and iCloud storage. If iCloud is unavailable, data may remain only on the current device. In that case, deleting the app may remove local data from that device.

To disable iCloud sync, open system iCloud settings, find SoloDay, and turn off the related permission. Data stored in iCloud is also subject to Apple's Privacy Policy and iCloud terms.

## 5. Third-Party Services

SoloDay may use the following third-party or system services to provide necessary features:

### 5.1 Apple iCloud

Used to sync SoloDay data across your Apple devices.

### 5.2 Apple WeatherKit

On supported iOS versions, SoloDay may use Apple WeatherKit to fetch weather information.

### 5.3 Open-Meteo

In some cases, SoloDay may use Open-Meteo to fetch weather forecast information. Weather requests may include latitude and longitude so the service can return weather results for the relevant location.

### 5.4 Firebase Analytics

SoloDay uses Firebase Analytics for basic analytics. Firebase Analytics may process device information, app usage information, event data, approximate location, or diagnostic information automatically collected by the SDK. Please refer to Google/Firebase privacy policies for more details.

### 5.5 Apple StoreKit And System Sharing

SoloDay may use Apple-provided system capabilities such as native review prompts, App Store links, Live Activities, notifications, widgets, and the share sheet. These capabilities are provided by the operating system and are subject to Apple's privacy policies.

## 6. What We Do Not Do

SoloDay does not:

- Publicly display your daily intentions, reflections, tasks, or Future notes to other users
- Sell your personal data
- Show third-party ads in the app
- Provide social feeds, leaderboards, or public check-ins
- Send exported content to anyone unless you actively choose a destination through the system share sheet

## 7. Permissions

SoloDay may request the following system permissions:

- Location permission: used for local weather, sunrise and sunset, UV, and precipitation information
- Notification or Live Activities permission: used for reminders, focus timer, Lock Screen state, or Dynamic Island state
- iCloud permission: used to sync data across your devices

You can manage or disable these permissions at any time in system settings. Disabling certain permissions may affect the related features.

## 8. Data Retention And Deletion

SoloDay keeps necessary data while you continue using the app so it can provide records, sync, review, and preference features.

You can manage or delete data by:

- Deleting specific tasks, Future notes, intentions, or reflections in the app
- Exporting content you want to keep as a backup
- Turning off SoloDay's iCloud sync in system settings
- Deleting the app to remove local app data
- Managing app data in system iCloud settings if you want to remove related iCloud data

Please note: if data has not been synced to iCloud, deleting the app may permanently remove local data from the current device.

## 9. Data Security

SoloDay uses system-provided security capabilities where available, including device local storage, iCloud sync mechanisms, and system permission controls. However, no electronic storage or network transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure, so we cannot promise that data security risks will never occur.

We recommend enabling a device passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID, and keeping your system and SoloDay updated to the latest version.

## 10. Children's Privacy

SoloDay is intended for general users and is not directed specifically to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to SoloDay without guardian consent, please contact us using the information below.

## 11. International Processing

Because iCloud, Firebase Analytics, weather services, and system services may be operated by providers in different countries or regions, your information may be processed outside your country or region. We aim to use these services only as necessary to provide app functionality.

## 12. Changes To This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when app features change, legal requirements change, or services are adjusted. When we update it, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this document. If changes are significant, we may notify you in the app or by other reasonable means.
