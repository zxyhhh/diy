Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
--------------------------------------------------

Q: How do I invite the other parent?
A: You can start recording on your own in Solo Mode. When you're ready to share, generate an invite code in the app and send it to the other parent. Once they enter the code, they join the same family and shared, confirmed record-keeping begins.

Q: Why is a new expense marked "Pending"?
A: New shared expenses start as Pending and wait for the other parent's review. They can approve or dispute it, with a reason on record. A record only affects the balance once both parents agree.

Q: Can approved records be edited or deleted?
A: To keep records trustworthy, approved and disputed records cannot be silently changed or deleted, and every key action is timestamped. This is by design for low-trust situations, ensuring a fair shared record.

Q: Why isn't a support payment counted in the balance right away?
A: Child support and alimony payments are counted only after the recipient confirms them, to avoid disputes from one-sided bookkeeping.

Q: I'm not receiving push notifications. What should I do?
A: Please check: (1) notifications are enabled for KidLedger in your system settings; (2) your notification preferences are on under Profile; (3) your network connection is working. If it still doesn't work, sign out and back in to refresh push settings, or contact us.

Q: How do I export my expense records?
A: Go to Profile, then Export Expenses, to generate a PDF report or export a CSV for any date range, clear enough for lawyers, mediators, or your own records.

Q: Does KidLedger process real payments?
A: No. KidLedger is a record-keeping and coordination tool. It does not process real payments and does not provide legal advice. It is designed to reduce conflict between co-parents.

Q: How do I delete my account?
A: Go to Profile, then Delete Account. Your account data will be removed after deletion. This action cannot be undone.
